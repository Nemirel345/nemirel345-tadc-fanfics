Chapter 6

When the Code Cries

Daisy: Hello?

Daisy stands on the black-and-white checkered floor of the circus. The place is dark, except for the area immediately surrounding her.

Daisy: Mom? Dad?

No answer.

She walks toward the edge of the light, but she’s still the same distance from the darkness.

Daisy: Okay. This isn’t funny.

She starts running. The tiles blur under her feet.

In the distant darkness, she sees a silhouette.

Daisy: Who’s there?

The silhouette doesn’t react.

She carefully approaches.

Daisy: Who are you? Where are we?

Still no answer.

When Daisy gets close enough that the figure enters the lit area, she recognizes it.

It’s… her!

It’s a copy of her, immobile, stuck in this pose. Arms and legs spread. Blank expression.

Daisy: What… is this?

She turns around.

Daisy: Caine? Mom? Dad? Anyone?

Pomni: Daisy?

Daisy: Mom?

She jolts upright in her bed.

Pomni: Daisy? Are you okay sweetie?

Pomni opens the door to Daisy’s room. Jax stays by the doorframe.

Jax: Dee? Hey, hey, we’re here.

Pomni: You’re okay. You’re okay. I’ve got you.

Daisy breathes heavily.

Daisy: I… I think I had a bad dream.

Jax: Loud enough to wake the entire circus, kiddo.

The walls of her room flicker. For an instant they become see-through, exposing the geometry behind.

Daisy: Did you see that?

Jax: See what?

Another flicker.

Jax: Okay, that one I saw.

Daisy: What’s going on? Why are the walls turning invisible? Why’s it doing that?

Pomni sits on her bed, closer.

Pomni: Hey, look at me, not at the wall.

Daisy: The wall went away!

Jax comes on the other side, softer now.

Jax: It didn’t go away. It… blinked. Happens sometimes.

Daisy: That’s not something walls are supposed to do.

Jax: Around here it is. Sometimes, kinda.

Another flicker.

Daisy: Make it stop!

Pomni reaches for Daisy’s hand.

Pomni: Squeeze my hand, okay? Just keep your eyes on me.

They breathe together. The flickering slows down, then pauses.

Jax watches the wall, then Daisy.

Pomni: See? It’s already calming down. You’re not in the dream anymore.

Daisy: Feels like the dream.

Jax: Yeah, but this time you’ve got backup.

Daisy: Why is it doing that? Really?

Pomni hesitates.

Pomni: This place… acts up, sometimes. Little flashes. Glitches. It’s not your fault.

Daisy: You said that before… glitches. Is this because this place is… digital?

Jax and Pomni both go still, then look at each other.

Jax: You’ve been listening, huh.

Daisy: You say that all the time. Digital this, digital that. Digital Circus. What does it mean?

Pomni: Daisy…

Daisy: Does digital mean… broken?

Another flicker. Pomni tightens her grip.

Pomni: No. No, it doesn’t mean broken.

Daisy: Then, why can I see through my walls?

Jax lets out a slow breath.

Jax: Okay, short version. You know how Caine can snap his fingers and change stuff? Make whole rooms into something else?

Daisy: Yeah.

Jax: Digital is the fancy word for how he does that. This place, it’s made of… rules, and pictures. Sometimes, the pictures stutter.

Daisy: Like my dream?

Pomni: Kinda like your dream. But you’re awake now, so it’s different.

Daisy: So the walls aren’t real?

Pomni: Real enough you can’t walk through… normally.

Jax: I tried. Zero out of ten, can’t recommend.

Daisy: But they can disappear.

Jax: They can… slip. For a second, then they’re back.

Another stutter. Daisy flinches.

Daisy: I don’t like it.

Pomni: You don’t have to like it. You just have to remember it’s not here to hurt you.

Daisy: How do you know?

Pomni: Because it’s happened before and we’re still here.

Jax: Annoyingly so.

Pomni shoots him a look.

Pomni: Hey, Dee. Look at me.

She does.

Pomni: Digital just means this is how the circus is built. Weird walls, weird rules, weird… everything.

She nods.

Pomni: Better?

Daisy: A little.

Caine: HELLO, non-scheduled nighttime scene!

Daisy jerks.

Daisy: Aah!

The walls flicker again.

Jax: Great. Of course.

Pomni: Caine, what are you doing in Daisy’s room?

Caine: I noticed some localized instability in this room. I thought I’d pop in and patch it.

He snaps his fingers. The wall flicker stops, then starts slowly coming back.

Caine: Huh.

Pomni: Huh what?

Caine: That usually works.

Jax: Super comforting.

Daisy presses closer to Pomni.

Daisy: Is it breaking?

Caine: Breaking? No, no. Just a little hiccup. Nothing for you to worry about.

He flicks his cane, which turns into a clipboard.

Caine: Let’s see… according to my very accurate logs, this is… not my fault. Though it appears this room is currently unassigned.

Daisy: But it’s my room.

Jax: Yeah, that’s on me. I popped it open when we needed a place for her to sleep. You were busy.

Bubble: Unauthorized onboarding. Naughty.

He taps his clipboard. A grid ripples through the walls and ceiling, then fades.

Caine: There, should be fixed now.

Daisy: So the room was glitching because it wasn’t… done?

Caine: The room is fine, just had to register it.

Pomni lays her head on Daisy’s.

Pomni: See, Dee? It wasn’t you, it’s just the Circus being slow.

Daisy: Then why’s the door still wrong?

Caine: Door?

Jax motions over to the open door.

Jax: Yeah, stock mannequin picture’s still on it.

Daisy: Everyone else has their face.

Caine: Door icons are a separate system. One I… don’t fully have control of. I’ll see what I can do.

Pomni: We’ll bug him about it later… make it official.

Jax: Or we tape one of your drawings on top of it.

Daisy: Maybe…

The walls flicker again.

Pomni: This has happened before, Caine. When you crash out.

Caine: I don’t remember that.

Pomni: You never do. You just snap back, everything’s fine again and it sounds like you forgot the last minute or so.

Daisy: So this is because you’re sad?

Jax: Or he hears something he doesn’t approve of.

Caine: What? No! Totally unrelated to anything you might call a mood.

Something drags along the floor in the doorway.

Zooble: Okay. Who’s screaming and why are the walls doing that?

Zooble appears, arms where their legs should be. No antennae, chest decor or wings. Minimal parts.

Zooble: Do you have any idea how far my room is?

Jax: You could have put your legs on.

Zooble: I was asleep, got out in a hurry.

The wall on the left flickers briefly. Zooble stares.

Zooble: Yeah… no. That’s one of his things.

Caine: Excuse you?

Zooble: Last time you crashed, the entire circus was doing the same thing.

Jax: Ha! See? Told you.

Caine throws his arms up.

Caine: Look… Whatever this is, I am patching it. I am on it. I am heroically attending to the sudden wall transparency issues and will have it fixed in… some amount of time that is definitely soon.

Pomni: And if it happens again?

Caine: Then it happens again and I fix it… again.

Jax: You sure it’s not spreading?

Caine: Absolutely… probably.

Zooble: You’re not gonna forget about this one, are you?

Caine: Of course not!

Zooble: You forgot the last few.

The last flicker stabilizes.

Caine: There. See? Perfectly stable. Not haunted.

Daisy: It’s not haunted.

Caine: Exactly! That’s the spirit… or the lack thereof. If anything else goes wooOOoo, you tell and I’ll come and make it nice and boring again.

Jax: You’ll remember you said that?

Caine: Absolutely! Maybe. Goodnight!

He disappears in a puff of smoke.

The room stays quiet. Zooble stares at the ceiling.

Zooble: Yeah… I don’t like that.

Jax: Which part?

Zooble: The localized bit. Last time he glitched out, it was everywhere. This is new.

They all carefully avoid looking at Daisy.

Daisy: Is it over?

Pomni squeezes her hand.

Pomni: For tonight, yeah.

Jax: And if it’s not, we’re still here.

Zooble snorts.

Zooble: I’m not dragging myself back to my room tonight… so if it starts again, I’m here too.

Pomni mouths a silent Thank you to Zooble. Daisy gives a tiny, tired smile.

Daisy: Maybe we can put my drawing on the door tomorrow.

Jax: Deal!
