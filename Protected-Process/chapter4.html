Chapter 4

Toddler Trouble in the Tent

Pomni: Stay still, Daisy!

Pomni wrestles with the buckle. Daisy plants her feet on the seat and pushes up.

Daisy: No! No stroller. I walk!

Pomni: Are you sure?

Daisy: I big now.

Jax: She’s got you there. Can’t argue with that logic.

Pomni: You taught her that line.

Jax: Maybe.

Pomni lets go of the belt.

Pomni: Fine. But you stay near. Understood?

Daisy: Undaswood.

Pomni: Good.

Pomni opens the door to her room.

Pomni: Come on then.

Daisy gets up from the stroller and bolts into the hallway.

Pomni: Stay near! I said stay near!

Jax: Look at that. Free-range toddler. Terrifying.

Daisy: Daddy, look! No wheels.

Jax: I see that, Dee. Very advanced.

Pomni reaches her hand out.

Pomni: Hand.

Daisy hesitates, then slips her hand into Pomni’s.

Daisy: Hand. I near.

Pomni: Good. You’re near, you’re safe. Now come on.

They walk down the hallway toward the common area. Daisy tugs Pomni forward with every step. Jax follows slightly behind, eyes on Daisy.

Pomni: Hey, slow down!

Daisy: But friends.

Jax: Friends aren’t going anywhere.

They reach the common area. Ragatha, Gangle, and Zooble lie on the couches. Kinger has sealed himself inside his impenetrable fortress.

Ragatha looks up first.

Ragatha: Oh… hey, Dee. On foot today?

Daisy: Hi Aunt Raggie. No stroller, I walk.

Ragatha: Big news!

Gangle waves.

Gangle: Hi Daisy.

Daisy: Hi Gangi!

Pomni slumps down onto the same couch as Ragatha. Jax leans against the armrest.

Ragatha: No stroller today, huh?

Pomni: Nope. Dee insisted she’s big enough to walk now.

Jax: Zooble must be so disappointed.

Zooble: FUCK off, Jax.

Jax: Sheesh, Zoobie. Language!

Zooble: If you two don’t need the stroller anymore, can I get those parts back?

Pomni: Oh, uh… Of course, Zooble. We’ll… drop it off in your room later.

Zooble: No rush, Pom.

The pillow fort rustles in the corner. Daisy approaches and knocks at the pillowy door.

Kinger: Who goes there? Friend or foe?

Daisy: House.

Kinger: A fortress, actually.

Kinger opens the door to the fort. The inside is lined with a ton of pillows.

Daisy rushes in.

Pomni: Dee! Get out of there! You didn’t ask permission to enter.

Kinger: Oh, it’s quite alright.

Daisy: I sowwy. Can I play in pillow house, Kingy?

Kinger: The pillow fortress isn’t for playing, Daisy. Maybe later, for your nap.

Daisy: No! No nap.

Zooble hands Gangle a bag of snacks. She takes one chip from the bag.

Daisy: Snack!

Pomni: Hey, no! You ask!

Daisy: Can I have snack, Zoob?

Ragatha: What do we say?

Daisy: Please.

Zooble pulls out one chip and hands it to Daisy.

Zooble: Just one.

She happily takes it and shoves it into her mouth.

Daisy: Fank you.

She turns to Pomni, lifting her arms in the air.

Daisy: Up! Up!

Ragatha: Come here, you.

Ragatha picks her up and sits her down between her and Pomni, her little legs pointing straight forward.

Daisy leans onto Pomni.

Ragatha: Sleepy already?

Daisy: No.

Daisy yawns.

Jax: Look at that. Five minutes of walking and she’s already wiped.

Pomni: You try being three in this place.

Daisy wriggles.

Daisy: Play?

Pomni: Later. We just sat down.

Jax: Toddlers don’t do later.

Daisy slides herself down the couch.

Pomni: Dee, stay close, okay?

Daisy: Okay.

Ragatha: You’re doing good, you know.

Jax: She’s fine. There’s nothing sharp in here. Besides Zoobie’s personality.

Zooble: And some of the parts I’m gonna shove up your—

Pomni: Okay!

Gangle: It… is nice seeing her walk. Makes it feel more… normal, I guess.

Ragatha: Yeah, normal.

They all look tired for a moment.

Pomni blinks and looks down. Daisy isn’t there anymore.

Pomni: Dee?

Nothing. She looks on the other side of the couch. Empty.

Pomni: Dee?

Ragatha: I thought she was with you.

Pomni: She was. I… She was right here!

Jax: She’s probably bothering Kinger.

Jax crouches next to the pillow fort.

Jax: You got a stowaway in there?

Kinger: No intruders! My defences are impenetrable… no toddler either, I would have noticed.

Pomni’s voice tightens.

Pomni: Daisy? Daisy!

No answer.

Gangle: She can’t have gone far. Right? Right.

Zooble: I’ll check the hall.

Jax: She’s fast, but not that fast.

Ragatha peeks behind the other couch.

Ragatha: Not here.

Gangle drops to her knees and looks under the coffee table.

Gangle: Not here either.

Pomni: Daisy! Answer Mommy!

A small voice, very close.

Daisy: Here.

Pomni: Where’s here?

Pomni looks down. Daisy sits just outside the common area, behind a wall.

Pomni exhales so hard her shoulders drop.

Pomni: Oh my god, Daisy!

She drops to her knees.

Pomni: What are you doing out here?

Daisy looks up, calm.

Daisy: Floor.

Pomni: You scared me. You need to tell me if you’re going somewhere.

Daisy: I near.

Jax peeks around the corner.

Jax: She’s not wrong. Technically.

Pomni: Not helping!

Pomni scoops Daisy into her arms.

Pomni: You stay where I can see you, understand?

Daisy: Undaswood.

Pomni presses her forehead to Daisy’s for a second.

Pomni: You’re trouble.

Jax: She gets it from you.

Pomni huffs a laugh.

Pomni: Come on, Dee. Back to the couch.

Daisy: Okay, Mommy.

They step back to the common room. Ragatha and Gangle both relax visibly.

Ragatha: Found her?

Pomni: She was right outside.

Pomni’s grip tightens just a little.

Pomni: Right where I couldn’t see her.

Pomni sits back down, Daisy still in her arms.

Daisy rests her head on Pomni’s shoulder.

Ragatha: You gave us all a scare, Dee.

Daisy yawns.

Daisy: I near.

Jax: Near isn’t the same as visible.

Daisy mumbles into Pomni’s shoulder.

Daisy: No nap. Play.

Pomni: You’re literally falling asleep.

Jax: Welcome to toddlers. They’re never tired until they are.

Pomni strokes Daisy’s ears.

Pomni: Okay, five minutes of no nap, then nap.

Daisy’s eyes close.

Daisy: O…kay.
