Chapter 5

The Question Game

Caine: Time!

Caine trumpets from nowhere.

Daisy hangs upside-down from the monkey bars, ears dangling.

Caine: Our reigning CHAMPION of the Jungle Gym of Mildly Concerning Perils has done it again. Seven minutes, twelve seconds, and only three near-fatal mishaps.

Bubble: A personal best!

Daisy drops and lifts her arms in the air.

Daisy: Ta-da!

Pomni claps like she’s at her kid’s school recital.

Pomni: That was amazing, Dee!

Jax gives a slow golf clap.

Jax: You skipped the part where you almost face-planted into the acid pit… again.

Daisy: It’s not real acid, Dad.

Jax: Yeah, but you didn’t know that when you screamed.

Daisy: I wasn’t screaming, I was… being dramatic.

Pomni: You definitely get that from your dad.

Jax: I’m a terrible influence.

Pomni: You absolutely are.

Daisy spins in a circle, arms out.

Daisy: Can we do something else now? Something new. Not the Jungle Gym. I already beat it.

Caine: ALWAYS hungry for novelty, our little daredevil. I LIKE it!

Caine snaps his fingers. A trampoline appears nearby.

Pomni: Can we chill on the perils for today?

Daisy: But I’m not even a little dead.

Pomni: Let’s try to keep it that way.

Daisy: Can I go on it? Can I? Can I please?

Pomni: Fine, but be careful, Dee.

Daisy climbs onto the trampoline and starts jumping around, careful to stay in the center.

Jax: Relax, Pom. She can’t get herself hurt here. Not really.

Pomni: I know, it’s just… I… It’s still scary, you know.

Daisy lands, bends her knees, and propels herself into another bounce.

Daisy: This is great! Why didn’t we do this sooner?

Pomni: Because the trampoline wasn’t here before, Dee.

Jax: Or the Jungle Gym.

Daisy: So, where did you go to play before?

Pomni: Before?

Daisy: Yeah. Before me? You had to play somewhere. When you were little, where was your Jungle Gym?

Jax snorts.

Jax: Oh, I was absolutely not allowed anywhere near anything with the word Peril in it. All we had was a sad little park behind the apart-

Pomni shoots him a glare.

Jax: Behind the uh… Circus. Totally. Sad little circus park.

Daisy slows her bouncing.

Daisy: What’s an apart?

Jax: An apart… ment. It’s just a boring kind of room.

Daisy: So you two played in the apart-room park?

Pomni: Something like that.

Daisy: Was I there?

Pomni and Jax exchange a look.

Daisy: In the apart-room place. Was I there too?

Jax: No, Dee. You weren’t… around yet.

Daisy: Because I wasn’t born?

Jax: Right.

Daisy’s ears tilt.

Daisy: Where was I, before I was born?

Pomni exhales.

Pomni: You were… nowhere yet, sweetie.

Daisy: Like the Void?

Pomni: No. Not like the Void. Just… nowhere.

Daisy stops jumping completely and sits on the edge of the trampoline.

Daisy: So, you two were in the apart-room place, and I was nowhere. Then you came to the circus and then… I was born here?

Pomni: That kinda sums it up, yeah.

Jax: Very compressed timeline.

Pomni: It’s not wrong, though.

Pomni takes a deep breath.

Pomni: There was… a place. Before the Circus. And before you. And then… the Circus. Then we had you.

Daisy: So there was a before-place and a before-me.

Pomni: Daisy-

Daisy: What was it like, the before-place?

Pomni swallows.

Pomni: I… don’t want to talk about that right now.

Daisy: Why?

Pomni: Because thinking about the before-place hurts.

Daisy: More than the acid?

Pomni: Different hurt.

Daisy disembarks the trampoline, goes to Pomni, and hugs her.

Daisy: If there was a before-place, can we go back?

Pomni sighs.

Pomni: No.

Daisy: Why not?

Jax: We’re stuck here.

Pomni: JAX!

Jax: It’s true.

Daisy: We’re stuck in the Circus?

Pomni: Kinda. We haven’t found a way to leave… yet.

Daisy: Is that why it hurts to talk about the before-place?

Pomni nods.

Pomni: Enough questions for now, okay?

Daisy holds back her next question.

Daisy: Okay.

Daisy lies on her back on her bed, feet kicking the air, ears flopped over the edge.

Daisy: Tell me the story of how you two met.

Pomni: When we…

Daisy: Yeah, like in the books. Once upon a time, there was a mom and a dad, and they met.

Jax: Oh, that disaster.

Pomni: It was not a disaster. That came later that day.

Jax: You were screaming.

Pomni: I just got sucked into this place. Screaming was reasonable.

Daisy’s eyes widen.

Daisy: You screamed at Dad when you met him?

Pomni: I screamed at everything on my first day in the Circus.

Daisy: So you met here?

Pomni: Yeah. Big day it was. Caine doing his thing, your dad being annoying.

Jax: Consistent character.

Daisy’s ear twitches.

Daisy: But, before the Circus, did you two meet in the before place?

The room quietens.

Pomni: No. We didn’t know each other then.

Daisy: But you were both in the apart-room place. Right?

Jax: I was in… an apartment. She was in a different one.

Pomni: Different lives, different places. Different… everything.

Daisy: And you never bumped into each other?

Jax: Pretty sure I’d remember bumping into a screaming jester girl.

Pomni: I wasn’t a jester then…

Daisy: What were you?

Pomni’s mouth opens, but she stops herself.

Pomni: Someone else. Different.

Daisy: So, you two only met after you got trapped in the circus, and then later I was born?

Pomni: Right.

Daisy: So then…

She sits up onto her bed, ears perked.

Daisy: How did I get made?

Jax and Pomni exchange a look.

Jax: That’s… a big question.

Daisy: Is it like crafts?

Pomni: Crafts?

Daisy: Yeah, like when you glue two things together to make a new thing. Did you glue yourselves into a me?

Jax chokes on a laugh.

Jax: Please never describe it that way again.

Pomni: It’s… more complicated than glue.

Daisy scoots to the edge of her bed, swinging her feet.

Daisy: So how are babies made?

Pomni: That’s… a conversation for when you’re older.

Daisy: I’m basically ancient!

Jax: You’re seven.

Daisy: Seven and a half.

Jax: Oh, my mistake. Practically fossilized.

Daisy: I can handle it.

Pomni: I know you can. I can’t.

Daisy rubs her eyes.

Daisy: How old are you?

Jax: Rude.

Pomni: I suppose, by my calculations, I’d be somewhere around one thousand three hundred-

Daisy: One thousand?

Jax whispers to Pomni.

Jax: Is that in weeks?

Pomni: Yeah. Putting it in her time scale.

Daisy: Stop whispering, you two! I can hear, you know.

Jax ruffles Daisy’s long ears.

Pomni: Look. In the before-place, there were… rules.

Daisy: Rules to making babies?

Jax: Yeah, really traumatizing if you learned how too early.

Daisy: What are the rules here?

Pomni: We… don’t know.

Daisy: But I’m here now.

Pomni: You are.

Daisy: So something must have happened. I didn’t come from the before-place and get stuck here like you did.

Pomni: It’s… complicated.

Daisy: Did I start as a tiny baby here?

Daisy cups her hands together, approximating something small.

Jax: More like… this big.

Jax moves her hands apart slightly.

Daisy: Do you remember me getting bigger, from when I was little-little?

Pomni’s expression softens.

Pomni: Yeah, we do.

Jax: First words, first steps, first time you bit me…

Daisy: You put your hand in front of my mouth!

Jax: Still an important milestone.

Daisy slumps back down on her bed.

Daisy: So you’re not gonna tell me how babies are made in the before-place?

Pomni: Absolutely not!

Daisy: Why?

Pomni: Because you’re too young. And I’m too… me.

Jax: For once, I’m in agreement with your mother here.

Daisy: But if I don’t know the rules, how do I know if I’m normal?

Pomni’s shoulders drop. Jax’s ears flop down.

Pomni: You are normal.

Daisy: But you won’t tell me the rules.

Pomni: We will. Someday. When it won’t make my brain explode.

Jax: And I can make it sound less horrifying.

Daisy crosses her arms.

Daisy: Grown-up rules are dumb.

Jax: Correct.

Pomni: No. They’re just… timed.

Daisy: Fine, keep your secret glue rules.

Jax snorts.

Jax: Well, that’s not leaving my brain, ever.

Daisy stares at the ceiling.

Daisy: When I’m older, I’m gonna ask again.

Pomni: I know.

Daisy: And this time, you’re not allowed to say not today.

Pomni: We’ll see.

Daisy: That’s not an answer.

Pomni: It’s the only one I’ve got right now.
