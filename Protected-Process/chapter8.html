Chapter 8

Mirror Maze

Jax: You know what gets me?

Jax lays upside down on the couch of the common room. The others are nearby. Daisy sits next to Pomni with her knees to her chest.

Jax: We live in a magic circus with a reality-bending ringmaster, and we still haven’t seen a single rollercoaster.

Ragatha: Please don’t give him ideas.

Jax: No, seriously. Caine, do you have one, or is this a low-budget circus?

Pomni hesitates.

Pomni: Wait. When I first got here… he did mention something like that. A… carnival? I think? I wasn’t really listening, I was too busy trying to find the exit.

You missed the part where we told you there wasn’t any.

Pomni: I got there in the end.

Jax perks up.

Jax: Hold on. We have a whole carnival and he’s been hoarding it this whole time?

Kinger: Oh yeah, terrible.

Ragatha sighs.

Ragatha: Oh. Oh, that. Right. That was… a day.

Pomni: You’ve been before?

Ragatha: Long time ago. Before all of you.

Jax throws his arms in the air.

Jax: Caine. Buddy. Pal. Are you seriously sitting on a carnival and feeding us Gloinks instead?

Caine pops out of nowhere right next to Jax’s ear.

Caine: Sitting on is such a weird way to put it.

Jax yelps and nearly falls off the couch.

Jax: Gah! Personal space, denture boy.

Caine: You make it sound like I forgot about it. I did NOT forget.

Jax: So there is a carnival?

Caine: Of course there’s a carnival! This is a circus. There are standards.

Jax: And you just… never took us?

Caine: I mentioned it! Repeatedly! We’ve got a lovely little carnival on the grounds, I said. Lots of attractions, I said. Did any of you say Gee, Caine, let’s go there? Nooo.

Pomni: I mean… I’ve only heard you mention it the one time.

Caine: And now, inspired by Jax’s excellent suggestion…

He pats Jax on the head.

Jax: I regret asking.

Caine: …I am proud to announce… a GRAND REOPENING!

He snaps his fingers. The ground slides underneath everyone, and they’re standing at the entrance a second later.

Caine: Come experience the Digital Carnival. Now with 5.7 times more thrilling safety violations.

Pomni: That seems… uhhh.

Jax: Nah! That’s perfect! Look at the drop on that one.

Caine: Today’s a free-roam day! No quests, just… FUN™.

Pomni: What do you think, Dee?

Daisy: It’s… whatever.

Pomni studies her for a second.

Pomni: We don’t have to do anything right away, we can just… walk?

Daisy: Walking’s fine.

Jax: Walk. Scout. Find the worst idea with a seatbelt… sounds like a plan!

They step through the archway into the main lane. There are rides and stalls on each side.

Jax’s eyes lock on the tallest coaster at the back.

Gangle spots a cork shooting game. She beams when she notices the prizes.

Gangle: Hey, Zooble. You have to win me a prize!

She wraps her ribbon around their wrist and drags them towards the stall.

Zooble: Alright. You really want me to embarrass everyone here?

Pomni: Don’t be modest. You’re a pretty good shot. Come to think of it, Kinger isn’t half bad either.

Kinger: Suppose I am.

Zooble and Kinger both pick up a cork rifle. Pomni, Jax and Daisy stand further back.

Jax: Kinger? Really?

Pomni: Yeah. Look at him go.

Jax glances over. Kinger is nailing targets, slightly behind Zooble’s score.

Jax: Please. I could do that in my sleep.

Pomni: You say that, but I’m pretty sure I’m a better shot than you.

Jax: What? Nah! That was one time, and it was pure luck.

Pomni: Sure. Luck.

Jax turns to Daisy.

Jax: Pick the ugliest thing in there and I’ll get it for you.

Daisy shrugs, eyes on the ground.

Daisy: I… don’t really want a plushie.

Jax: Doesn’t need to be a plushie, could be anything!

Daisy: I’m good.

Pomni’s smile drops a bit.

Pomni: Let’s… see what else there is. Okay?

Daisy: I guess.

Jax: Alright, no prizes for you, miss.

They take a few steps towards the attractions.

Behind them, a cheer goes up as Zooble knocks down the last target.

They turn and see Gangle being handed a large bear plushie.

Jax: See what you’re missing, Dee? Most people don’t say no to free stuffed toys.

Pomni shoots him a look. Daisy shrugs, already turning back.

They walk down the row of attractions.

Pomni: You’ve been… quiet today. More than usual. You feeling okay?

Daisy: I’m fine.

Jax: You don’t look fine. You look like Caine swapped you out with one of the mannequins.

Daisy: I said I’m fine!

Pomni flinches a little.

Pomni: You’re not mad about something, are you? With us?

Daisy: I’m not… mad.

Jax: That’s the least convincing not mad I’ve ever heard.

Daisy: I’m just… tired. And thinking. Can that be it for now?

Pomni: Alright. Just… if you wanna talk, you can. You know that, right?

Daisy gives a noncommittal nod.

Jax’s eyes skip over the attractions to the silhouette of the coaster at the back.

Jax: Okay. There it is. The big one. Look at that drop!

Pomni: Absolutely not! We’re not starting with that.

Jax: Come on, Pom, look at it. It’s beautiful.

Jax spreads his arms.

Jax: First ride of the day. You gotta start big.

Pomni: No. No you absolutely do not. We can start with something normal, like the carousel. Or, you know, literally anything else that doesn’t invert you.

Jax groans.

Jax: If we start with the baby rides, we’re never moving up.

Pomni: If we start with that, I’m never moving again.

Jax: Lame…

Pomni: We are NOT doing the loop first!

Jax: Then we do it second.

Pomni: Jax?

Jax: What?

Pomni: …Where’s Daisy?

Jax turns. He scans along the lane.

Jax: She’s right there. By the weird mirror place.

She’s standing in front of the entrance, just… staring at the sign.

Daisy: See yourself.

Pomni goes over.

Pomni: Dee, hey, you scared me.

Jax: Really? Out of all this… you pick the creepy mirror shack?

Daisy: I just… want to see.

Jax: See what?

Daisy: …Myself. I guess.

Pomni’s smile falters.

Pomni: We don’t have to start with this one. We can come back later, after we’ve tried something else. The carousel, or a smaller ride, or—

Daisy: I don’t want anything else. I want this.

She steps towards the entrance.

Pomni: Okay… If that’s what you want. We’ll stick together inside, alright?

Daisy gives a small nod. Pomni offers her hand but Daisy doesn’t grab it.

She pulls the curtain aside and steps in. They follow in after her.

Inside are mirrors and glass aligned in a crude maze.

Jax lets out a low whistle.

Jax: Oh wow! Mirrors and reflections. Truly the peak of entertainment. Great pick, Dee.

Pomni shoots him a look, then turns to Daisy.

Pomni: You said you were… thinking, earlier. Is that… about us?

Daisy: I said I’m fine.

Jax: Yeah, no. You’re clearly not. We’re not stupid. What’s going on?

Daisy’s shoulders tense. She stares at her reflection in one of the mirrors.

She exhales, long and shaky.

Daisy: The other day.

Jax: What about it?

Daisy: When you thought I was asleep. You were… in your room. You were talking. About me.

Pomni: You… heard that?

Daisy: I didn’t mean to. I was going to apologize for earlier, but then…

Pomni: What did you hear?

Daisy: Something about my glitching.

Pomni and Jax look at each other.

Pomni: It’s not—

Daisy: Don’t lie to me! I heard you say I was causing it, not Caine.

Pomni: We… don’t know that.

Daisy: I do!

She points at herself. Her reflection becomes choppy, trailing behind.

Daisy: See?

Pomni: It could just be the weird mirror.

Daisy: Then why is your reflection normal? Why is it just mine?

Pomni: Dee… I—

Daisy: Is it because… I’m an NPC?

Pomni’s jaw drops. She looks over at Jax. He has a similar expression.

Their silence confirms it for Daisy.

Pomni: Where… did you—

Daisy: So you’re not denying it.

Pomni: We didn’t know how to tell you.

Pomni reaches for Daisy’s shoulder.

Daisy pulls away and backs into a mirror. She slides down and hugs her knees.

Daisy: If I’m such trouble, did you even want me?

Pomni: You’re our daughter!

Daisy: You’re not answering my question.

Pomni sighs.

Pomni: We… weren’t really expecting you. Caine just—

Jax: Gave you to us. You just… showed up.

Daisy: So I’m just something he decided to hand to you. I’m not even your daughter.

Pomni: You are, Dee!

Pomni slowly approaches.

Daisy: Stop! Go away!

Pomni: Dee.

Daisy: I said GO AWAY!

She leans back against the mirror.

Only… she doesn’t feel it anymore. Instead, she feels a cold wave pass through her. And the sensation of falling. Backwards.

Then she hits the ground. Outside the hall of mirrors. She feels the gravel floor.

Daisy: Ow… What the FUCK?

Inside, Pomni and Jax look horrified as their daughter phases through the mirror.

One moment, she’s there. The next, just their own reflection.

Pomni: Daisy!

Pomni rushes to the mirror. She places her palms against it.

Jax pounds on the glass.

Jax: Damn-it!

Pomni: Dee!

Outside, Daisy gets on her feet, looks back, then bolts.

She runs into a dark, quiet part of the carnival, behind one of the attractions.

She’s crying, wiping her tears from her face.

She bumps her back against a wooden crate, slides down, and hugs her knees.

Silence.

Then, a voice from the other side of the crate.

Kinger: Careful, this crate has nails.

Daisy jumps.

Daisy: …Kinger?

Kinger: The one. Unless there’s more. I haven’t counted.

Daisy: I… didn’t know anyone else was here. I can leave if you want.

Kinger: Oh, no need. The crate can hold two disasters.

They stay silent for a bit. Daisy’s breath is shaky.

Daisy: They… lied to me.

Kinger: Classic.

Daisy: About what I am.

Kinger: Less classic. Go on.

Daisy: They have this word. For me. They kept saying it when they thought I wasn’t listening.

Daisy clenches her paw.

Daisy: NPC. They said I’m an NPC. Not to my face, but… they didn’t deny it. I don’t even know what that means, but I know it’s not like them. Not like you. Not like everyone else.

Kinger: Ah. Non-Player Character. I spent years making those.

Daisy: So you know.

Kinger: Oh yes.

Daisy: If I’m an NPC. That means I’m not real. I’m just something Caine created. Part of an adventure.

Kinger: That’s the short version. Easy to print on a label.

Daisy: Isn’t it the truth, though?

Kinger rounds the corner of the crate. He sits on the adjacent side to Daisy’s.

Kinger: Did that word make any of this hurt less?

Daisy: …No.

Kinger: Then it’s just the label.

Daisy turns her head to Kinger.

Daisy: Do you… think I’m real?

Kinger: I noticed since the day you showed up that you argue, sulk, and think like a person. That’s enough for me.

Daisy: But if Caine made me, doesn’t that make me less… something? Less real? I wasn’t born, I was just… created.

Kinger: Most NPCs walk their path, say their line, and vanish when they’re done. They don’t run away to a dark alley and cry against a crate.

Daisy: That doesn’t change what I am.

Kinger: No. You’re an NPC. You’re Daisy. You’re a kid. You think. You hurt. And you love two people who are terrible at honesty.

Daisy: That’s… a lot of things.

Kinger: And they’re all true. How you started doesn’t get to decide whether you’re real.

Daisy: It still feels like it means I’m not. Mom… I mean Pomni and Jax… they didn’t even ask for me. I was just… given to them.

Kinger: Caine may created you, but your mom and dad raised you. That’s family.

Daisy takes a deep breath.

Daisy: I still… feel like their kid.

Kinger: Because you are. They fed you, tucked you in, panicked about you, and loved you every day. You don’t do all that for a prop.

Daisy: I’m scared, you know. Of what I am.

Kinger: Only real people are scared of being fake. Fake ones don’t notice.

Daisy: They should have told me.

Kinger: Yes.

Daisy: I’m still mad.

Kinger: You should be. You’re allowed to be furious.

Daisy: I don’t know if I’m ready to talk to them.

Kinger: You don’t have to be ready. You just have to be curious about what they’ll say when you ask I know I’m an NPC. Does that make me less your daughter?

Daisy: Yeah… I wanna hear them say it.

Kinger: There you go.

Daisy: I don’t forgive them.

Kinger: Good. Take that with you. Start with I’m still mad. The rest will follow. Forgiveness is a dessert, not served while everything’s burning.

Daisy shifts over and hugs Kinger.

Daisy: Will you be okay here? You’re acting… different than usual.

Kinger: Oh yeah. The dark and I are long-time friends. I’ll be fine.

Daisy: Thank you.

She pushes herself to her feet. She wipes her tears and turns towards the faint lights of the carnival.
